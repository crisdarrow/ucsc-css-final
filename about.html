What Inspires Me

Logos and Icons

The most famous logos are simple, effective and make a lasting impression. The following is a list my favorite logos and icons:

  • Apple. The original logo was designed by Rob Janoff.[1] It had a rainbow background. The bite out of the apple was to let people know it was an apple and not a cherry. The bite also sounds like ‘byte’ - a unit of digital information.

  • The Coca-Cola logo was originialy created by Frank Mason Robinson in 1885. It has gone through many transformations but has essentially remained the same with the cursive font. [2] I love how the logo is recognized globally.

  • I can always spot a FedEx truck anywhere. I love the simplicity of the logo and how the hidden arrow between the ‘E’ and the ‘x’ symbolizes precision and speed.[3]

  • NBC. The original peacock dates back to May 22, 1956.[4] Its colors celebrated the start of colored programming and just plain makes me happy.

  • I love the simplicity of the Sochi 2014 Olympic icons otherwise known as pictograms. The designer (unknown) took inspiration from the icons designed by Nicolai Belkov for the Moscow 1980 Olympics [5] and Otl Aicher who was a German graphic designer and typographer. The thick, stick figure characters on thin, soft lines convey an inviting playfulness.
